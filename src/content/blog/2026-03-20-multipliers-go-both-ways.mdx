We always hear about how LLMs and AI agents are 2x, 5x, 10x, even 100x multipliers for great engineers. 

And while I tend to agree that these tools have enabled strong developers to really excel their output, I've been thinking about an old meme:

[insert Anakin Dooku meme]

Though no engineers on a team are (hopefully) a 0, they may not necessarily be a 1 either. This is not a dig at any engineer either. We have to work around the stack, and engineers are naturally stronger on some parts and weaker in others. A stellar backend Java dev will likely not also be a top frontend React dev, and vice versa.

With the rise of AI coding tools, I've seen an expansion in the type of work that engineers will take on, including me. Though I'm strongly specialized in frontend, I've been more exploratory in taking on certain Java tickets. I no longer have to wade through classes like `AbstractTransactionalSingletonFactoryProviderManagerConfigurationAdapterDelegateImpl`, Claude can comb through it and just let me know what needs to be done, then I do a check with the Java skills I developed from my first year CS course that I haven't used since 2014.

When you multiply a number between 0 and 1, regardless of how big the multiplier is (ignoring < 1x multipliers), it's going to get smaller.
